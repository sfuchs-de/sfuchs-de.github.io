Trade-induced Structural Change in a Spatial Framework: Empirical Evidence

Due to geographic differences some location might be better positioned to benefit from a temporary export boom. In a setting with scale economies the resultant labor reallocation and innovation might have long run influences even if the export boom is only temporary. I evaluate strength of this mechanism from a historical natural experiment.
